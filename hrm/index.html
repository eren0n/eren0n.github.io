Chapter 1

Introduction to Human Resource Management

What is HRM?

Core Definition
Human Resource Management (HRM) — İnsan Kaynakları Yönetimi — is the process of acquiring, training, appraising, compensating, and attending to employee relations, health, safety, and fairness concerns. It is embedded in the broader management process.

HRM is not just an HR department function — all managers are HR managers because every manager is involved in recruiting, interviewing, selecting, and training employees. The four functions of management are:

Planning
Setting objectives and deciding on actions
Organizing (Staffing)
Arranging the structure and filling positions
Leading
Directing and motivating employees
Controlling
Measuring and correcting performance

Key HRM Activities

  • Conducting job analysis (iş analizi)
  • Planning labor needs (iş gücü planlaması)
  • Acquiring talented employees — recruiting & selection (işe alım ve seçme)
  • Orienting (oryantasyon) and training new hires
  • Creating and administering performance appraisals (performans değerlendirmesi)
  • Managing wages and compensation (ücret yönetimi)
  • Attending to labor relations (işçi ilişkileri), health, safety, and fairness

Line vs. Staff Managers

Line Authority (Hat Yetkisi)
Right to issue orders, provide directions, establish rules. Line managers have direct HR duties: job placement, orientation & training, performance, cooperation, controlling labor costs, development.
Staff Authority (Kurmay Yetkisi)
Right to advise others in the organization. HR managers are primarily staff managers — they assist and advise line managers in recruiting, hiring, and compensation.
Key Point: The HR manager has three functions simultaneously: line function (directs HR dept), coordinative function (ensures policies are implemented), and staff function (advises the CEO and line managers).

Important Trends in HRM

Globalization (Küreselleşme)
Firms extending sales, ownership, and manufacturing abroad. Reasons: sales expansion, lower labor costs, forming partnerships, becoming more competitive.
Technological Trends
Smartphones, tablets, LinkedIn, Facebook recruiting, AI; call-center outsourcing (e.g., Dell → India); internet-based distribution networks.
Human Capital (İnsan Sermayesi)
The knowledge, education, training, skills, and expertise of a firm's workers. Today's best jobs go to the best knowledge workers.
Generation Y Workers
More family-centric or dual-centric (vs work-centric). High-performance and high-maintenance; seek fair & direct supervisors and creative challenges.
Generation Z Workers
30% of global population; 27% of workforce by 2025. Value diversity, equality, inclusion, transparent & fast pay, mental health, flexible culture.
Economic Trends
Inflation, interest rates, GNI, economic crises, and recessions all affect HR decisions.

The New HR Manager — Key Competencies

  • Big picture — understanding business strategy
  • Talent management (yetenek yönetimi)
  • Employee engagement (çalışan bağlılığı)
  • Ethics (etik)
  • Performance & evidence-based practice
  • Transactional services (işlemsel hizmetler)
Chapter 3

HRM Strategy and Analysis

Core Concepts

Strategic Management (Stratejik Yönetim)
The process of identifying and executing the organization's mission by matching its capabilities with the demands of its environment.
Strategy
A course of action; a company's long-term plan for how it will balance its internal strengths & weaknesses with external opportunities & threats to maintain competitive advantage.
Strategic Plan (Stratejik Plan)
A company's plan for how it will match internal strengths and weaknesses with external opportunities and threats. HR must be involved not just in execution but also in design.

Three Levels of Strategy

Corporate Strategy — "What businesses are we in?" (Konsantrasyon, çeşitlendirme, dikey entegrasyon, konsolidasyon, coğrafi genişleme)

Competitive Strategy — "How will we compete in each business?" (Cost leadership, differentiation, focus)

Functional Strategy — "How does each department (HR, Sales, Production) support the competitive strategy?"

Corporate Strategies — Types

Concentration (Konsantrasyon)
One product/product line, usually in one market.
Diversification (Çeşitlendirme)
Expanding by adding new product lines (e.g., PepsiCo, Unilever).
Vertical Integration (Dikey Entegrasyon)
Producing own raw materials OR selling directly to consumers (e.g., Apple Stores).
Consolidation (Konsolidasyon)
Reducing the company's size.
Geographic Expansion (Coğrafi Genişleme)
Growing by entering new (international) markets.

Competitive Strategies (Porter)

Cost Leadership (Maliyet Liderliği)
Compete on lowest cost / price.
Differentiation (Farklılaştırma)
Compete on unique product/service attributes.
Focus (Odaklanma)
Target a narrow market segment.

Strategic HRM

Definition
Strategic HRM — Stratejik İKY — involves the development of a consistent, aligned collection of practices, programs, and policies to facilitate the achievement of the organization's strategic objectives. It links HRM with strategic goals to improve business performance and develop cultures fostering innovation and flexibility.

Key shift: from operational / administrative "Personnel Management" thinking → to strategic HRM thinking.

HR's Roles in Strategy

  • Partner with senior & line managers in strategy execution
  • Expert in organizing work — delivering administrative efficiency
  • Champion for employees — representing their concerns to senior management
  • Agent of continuous transformation — shaping processes and organizational culture
  • Supply competitive intelligence and identify human strengths/weaknesses during strategy formulation

Strategic HRM Tools

HR Scorecard (İK Karnesi)
Measures the HR function's effectiveness in producing employee behaviors needed to achieve strategic goals. Requires knowing the strategy, causal links between HR activities and outcomes, and metrics for all activities.
HPWS (Yüksek Performans İş Sistemi)
High-Performance Work System. Practices include: high-involvement (job enrichment, team-based), high-commitment (employee development, communication), flexible work assignments, skilled workforces.
Strategic Fit (Stratejik Uyum)
All of the firm's activities must be tailored to/fit its strategy. Functional strategy of HR should fit and support the company's competitive aims.
Hierarchy of Goals: Strategic plans set by top management cascade down to departmental plans and individual goals — forming a hierarchy that guides everyone in the organization.

Competitive Advantage via HR

Competitive advantage (rekabet avantajı) = any factors that allow an organization to differentiate its product/service to increase market share. Superior human resources are an important source of competitive advantage because human capital is hard to duplicate.

Chapter 4

Job Analysis and the Talent Management Process

Core Definitions

Job Analysis (İş Analizi)
The procedure for determining the duties and skill requirements of a job and the kind of person who should be hired for it. It is the foundation of HRM — produces job descriptions and job specifications.
Job Description (İş Tanımı)
A written statement of what the worker actually does, how he/she does it, and what the job's working conditions are. Lists duties, responsibilities, reporting relationships, working conditions, and supervisory responsibilities.
Job Specification (İş Şartnamesi)
A list of a job's "human requirements" — requisite education, skills, personality, and so on. Answers: what kind of person to hire?

What Job Analysis Captures

  • Work activities — cleaning, selling, teaching, painting; how/why/when performed
  • Human behaviors — sensing, communicating, deciding, writing; physical demands (lifting, walking)
  • Machines, tools, equipment, work aids
  • Performance standards — quantity and quality levels expected
  • Job context — working conditions, schedule, organizational/social context
  • Human requirements — education, training, experience, personality, aptitudes

Uses of Job Analysis Information

Recruitment & Selection
Determines what the job entails and what human characteristics are required → informs what to recruit for.
Performance Appraisal (Performans Değerlendirmesi)
Determines specific activities and performance standards for evaluation.
Compensation (Ücretlendirme)
Crucial for estimating the value of each job (skill level, hazards, responsibility, etc.).
Training (Eğitim)
The job description shows activities and skills required → guides training design.

Conducting a Job Analysis — 6 Steps

  1. Decide how the information will be used
  2. Collect background information (organization charts, process charts)
  3. Select representative positions to analyze
  4. Collect and analyze data using various methods
  5. Verify the data with workers and supervisors
  6. Produce the job description and specification
Best Practice: Make job analysis a joint effort by HR manager, worker, and worker's supervisor. Use several different tools — don't rely only on questionnaires.

Methods of Collecting Job Analysis Information

MethodDescriptionAdvantagesDisadvantages
Interview Structured or unstructured oral questions to employees, supervisors Quick; captures overlooked info Distorted / biased information
Questionnaire Employees fill out structured checklists or open-ended forms Quick for large numbers of employees Expensive & time-consuming to prepare
Observation Analyst directly watches employee perform job tasks First-hand; reduces distortion Time-consuming; misses infrequent tasks; poor for mental work
Participant Diary/Log Workers keep chronological logs of activities and time spent Complete picture; employee participation Distortion; relies on accurate self-recall

Writing Job Descriptions — Key Sections

Job Identification
Job title, preparation date, prepared by.
Job Summary
General nature of the job; major functions or activities.
Relationships
Reports to (supervisor); Supervises; Works with (internal); Outside the company contacts.
Responsibilities & Duties
Essential functions; limits of decision-making authority; budgetary limitations.
Standards of Performance
Specific measurable standards expected (e.g., "work group produces no fewer than 426 units/day").
Working Conditions
Location, tools, environment (hot/cold, etc.).
Job Specifications
Human requirements: education, skills, personality, experience.

Job Specifications — Two Types

Trained Personnel (Deneyimli Aday)
Focus on: length of previous service, quality of relevant training, previous job performance.
Untrained Personnel (Deneyimsiz Aday)
Focus on: physical traits, personality, interests, or sensory skills implying potential.

Job Redesign Concepts

Job Enlargement (İş Genişletme)
Assigning workers additional same-level activities — increases the number of tasks performed.
Job Enrichment (İş Zenginleştirme)
Redesigning jobs to increase opportunities for responsibility, achievement, growth, and recognition — adds depth (vertical).
Job Rotation (İş Rotasyonu)
Moving a trainee/worker from department to department to broaden experience, identify strengths/weaknesses, and enhance work team performance.

De-Jobbing & Modern Organization

Rapid technological change, global competition, and demographic shifts are causing de-jobbing — moving away from narrowly defined job roles. Organizations respond with:

  • Boundaryless organizations — flat structures, team-based (e.g., GE, Walmart, IKEA)
  • Re-engineering — radical redesign of business processes
  • Broadbanding (geniş bant iş tanımları) — broader, more flexible job descriptions
  • Performance-based job descriptions
  • Empowered employees (güçlendirilmiş çalışanlar) & skills matrices
Chapter 5

Personnel Planning and Recruiting

The 5-Step Recruitment & Selection Process

  1. Decide what positions to fill through workforce planning and forecasting
  2. Build a pool of candidates by recruiting internal or external candidates
  3. Have candidates complete application forms and undergo initial screening
  4. Use selection tools (tests, background checks, physical exams) to identify viable candidates
  5. Decide who to make an offer to via supervisor interview(s)

Planning & Forecasting Concepts

Employment/Personnel Planning (Personel Planlaması)
The process of deciding what positions the firm will have to fill, and how to fill them.
Succession Planning (Halef Planlaması)
The process of deciding how to fill the company's most important executive jobs. Steps: identify key needs → develop inside candidates → assess and choose.

Forecasting Personnel Needs (Labor Demand)

Trend Analysis (Trend Analizi)
Study past employment needs over years to predict future needs.
Ratio Analysis (Oran Analizi)
Determine future staff needs using ratios between a causal factor and number of employees. Assumes the relationship is constant.
Scatter Plot (Dağılım Grafiği)
Graphical method to identify relationships between two variables (e.g., hospital beds vs. number of nurses needed).
Markov Analysis
A quantitative technique forecasting movement of employees from one position to another over time.

Forecasting the Supply of Candidates

Qualifications Inventories (Nitelik Envanteri)
Records listing employees' education, career interests, languages, special skills — used to select inside candidates for promotion.
Personnel Replacement Charts (Yedekleme Tablosu)
Company records showing present performance and promotability of inside candidates for important positions.
HRIS (İK Bilgi Sistemi)
Human Resource Information System — computerized inventory of employee background, experience, skills (experience codes, industry knowledge, formal education).

Internal Sources of Candidates

AdvantagesDisadvantages
Foreknowledge of candidates' strengths/weaknessesFailed applicants become discontented
More accurate view of candidate's skillsTime wasted interviewing unsuitable insiders
Stronger commitment to the companyInbreeding of the status quo (mevcut durumun sürdürülmesi)
Increases employee morale (çalışan morali)
Less training and orientation required
Job Posting (İş İlanı — İç)
Publicizing an open job to employees (bulletin boards, intranet) and listing its attributes.
Rehiring Former Employees
Pros: known quantities, know the firm. Cons: may have negative attitudes; wrong message to current employees.

External Sources of Candidates

Advertising (Reklam/İlan)
Media choices: newspapers, trade/professional journals, internet job sites. Effective ads use the AIDA principle: Attention → Interest → Desire → Action.
Employment Agencies (İş Ajansları)
Public (government-run), nonprofit-associated, or privately owned. Used when: no HR dept, difficulty generating applicant pool, urgent openings, diversity goals.
Executive Recruiters / Headhunters (Kafa Avcıları)
Seek out top-management talent. Contingent-based: paid only on successful hire. Retained: paid regardless of outcome.
College Recruiting (Üniversite Kampüs İşe Alımı)
Goals: determine if candidate is worthy of further consideration; attract good candidates. On-site visits: invitation, hosts, info package, planned interviews, timely offer, follow-up.
Employee Referrals (Çalışan Referansı)
Current employees recommend applicants. Cost-effective; referring employees become stakeholders; can speed up diversifying the workforce.
Internet Recruiting
Cost-effective; wider reach; immediate responses; online prescreening; automation of tracking. Growing use of LinkedIn, Facebook recruiting, virtual job fairs.

Application Forms

Provide information on education, prior work record, and skills. Used to judge: educational/experience qualifications, previous progress, employment stability (istihdam istikrarı), and predictions of future job success.

CV / Resume components: Personal info, contact info, educational background, achievements & awards, work experience, skills & competencies (software, languages), interests, certificates, references.
Chapter 6

Employee Testing and Selection

Why Careful Selection Matters

  • Performance — the right person does the job better
  • Cost — hiring mistakes are expensive
  • Legal obligations (yasal yükümlülükler) — fair hiring is required by law
  • Person-job/organization fit — match between person and role

Basic Testing Concepts

Reliability (Güvenilirlik)
Consistency of scores obtained by the same person when retested. Are the results stable over time?
Validity (Geçerlilik)
The accuracy with which a test measures what it purports to measure. Does the test actually measure what we need it to?
Criterion Validity (Kriter Geçerliliği)
Test scores (predictors) are statistically related to job performance (criterion). E.g., "Do scores on this test predict who will perform well?"
Content Validity (İçerik Geçerliliği)
Test contains a fair sample of tasks and skills actually needed for the job. E.g., "Does the test question actually relate to job tasks?"
Construct Validity (Yapı Geçerliliği)
Tests whether the test measures the theoretical construct it claims to (e.g., intelligence, leadership).

How to Validate a Test — 5 Steps

  1. Analyze the job — identify predictors (job spec) and criterion (measures of job success)
  2. Choose the tests — single test or test battery?
  3. Administer the test — concurrent validation (current employees' scores vs. current performance) OR predictive validation (prior test scores vs. later-measured performance)
  4. Relate test scores and criteria — correlation analysis of actual test scores vs. actual performance
  5. Cross-validate and revalidate — repeat steps 3 & 4 with a different sample

Types of Tests

Cognitive Ability Tests (Bilişsel Yetenek)
Intelligence tests (IQ); specific cognitive abilities (mechanical comprehension, numerical reasoning, spatial ability).
Motor & Physical Ability Tests
Measure physical strength, dexterity, coordination (relevant for manual jobs).
Personality Tests (Kişilik Testleri)
Measure traits like the Big Five. Used to predict performance and reduce turnover via personality profiling.
Interest Inventories (İlgi Envanteri)
Compare person's current interests with those of others in various occupations to determine preferred career path. Used for personal development and selection.
Achievement Tests (Başarı Testleri)
Measure what a person has already learned — "job knowledge" in areas like accounting, marketing, or personnel.

The "Big Five" Personality Traits

ExtraversionDışadönüklük

Tendency to be sociable, assertive, active, with positive energy and zeal.

Emotional StabilityDuygusal Denge (Nevrotizm)

Poor emotional adjustment → anxiety, insecurity, hostility. (Neuroticism = instability.)

OpennessDeneyime Açıklık

Imaginative, nonconforming, unconventional, autonomous.

AgreeablenessUyumluluk

Trusting, compliant, caring, and gentle.

ConscientiousnessSorumluluk Bilinci

Two facets: achievement orientation and dependability (güvenilirlik).

Work Simulations & Assessment

Management Assessment Center (Yönetim Değerlendirme Merkezi)
A simulation in which management candidates perform realistic tasks in hypothetical situations and are scored. Exercises include: in-basket (gelen kutusu), leaderless group discussion, management games, individual presentations, objective tests, interviews.
Situational Judgment Tests (Durumsal Yargı Testleri)
Candidates choose how they would respond to job-related scenarios.
Miniature Job Training & Evaluation
Candidates are trained to perform a sample of job tasks, then evaluated. Assumes: someone who can learn and perform a sample can learn and perform the whole job.
Realistic Job Previews (Gerçekçi İş Önizlemeleri)
Give candidates an honest picture of the job (positive and negative aspects) to reduce turnover caused by unrealistic expectations.
Chapter 7

Interviewing Candidates

Types of Interviews by Purpose

Selection Interview (Seçme Mülakatı)
Designed to predict future job performance based on applicants' oral responses. The main type discussed in HRM selection.
Appraisal Interview (Değerlendirme Mülakatı)
Discussion following a performance appraisal; supervisor and employee discuss the rating and possible remedial actions.
Exit Interview (Çıkış Mülakatı)
Conducted when an employee leaves; provides insight into what's right or wrong about the firm.

Interview Structure

TypeDescriptionKey Feature
Unstructured (Non-directive) Manager follows no set format; questions come to mind spontaneously Flexible but inconsistent; less reliable
Structured (Directive) Questions listed in advance; possible answers may also be scored All applicants get same questions → more reliable and valid. Generally superior.
Sequential Multiple interviewers in sequence, each rates candidate independently Structured sequential: standardized evaluation forms
Panel Interview Team of interviewers interview each candidate simultaneously; combine ratings Final panel score from multiple raters

Types of Interview Questions

Situational (Durumsal Sorular)
Focus on how the candidate would behave in a given hypothetical situation. "What would you do if...?"
Behavioral (Davranışsal Sorular)
Focus on how the candidate reacted to actual past situations. "Tell me about a time when..." Based on the premise that past behavior predicts future behavior.
Job-related (İşe İlişkin Sorular)
Focus on relevant past job-related behaviors and experience.
Background Questions
About work experience, education, qualifications. "How much experience have you had with...?"
Stress Interview (Stres Mülakatı)
Interviewer intentionally makes applicant uncomfortable with rude/challenging questions to spot low or high stress tolerance.
Puzzle Questions
Pose unusual problems requiring creative ("out-of-the-box") solutions to see how candidates think under pressure. Used in technical and finance roles.

Interview Formats / Delivery Methods

  • Phone interviews — can be more accurate than face-to-face for judging conscientiousness, intelligence, and interpersonal skills
  • Video/web-assisted (Skype, iPad) — growing use
  • Computerized interviews — candidate responds to questions/situations via computer
  • Second Life / virtual — Microsoft and HP have used online virtual communities
  • Speed dating model — candidates visit briefly with selected employees
  • Case interviews — candidate solves a business problem

Errors that Undermine Interviews

Snap Judgments (Ani Kararlar)
Interviewers jump to conclusions within the first few minutes. Negative bias: unfavorable info influences more than positive info.
Misunderstanding the Job (İşi Yanlış Anlama)
Not knowing what the job entails → decisions based on incorrect stereotypes of a good applicant.
Candidate-Order Error (Aday Sırası Hatası)
Rating of a candidate affected by the quality of candidates interviewed just before. A mediocre candidate after poor candidates seems better than they are.
Nonverbal Behavior & Impression Management
Interviewers infer personality from nonverbal cues — has large impact on ratings. Clever candidates manage their impression deliberately (izlenim yönetimi).
Personal Characteristics Bias
Less favorable view toward candidates who are physically unattractive, female, of a different racial background, or disabled.
Interviewer Behaviors
Inadvertently telegraphing expected answers; talking too much; letting applicant dominate; acting more positively toward a favored/similar applicant.

How to Conduct an Effective Interview — 8 Steps

  1. Know the job — study the job description beforehand; understand what human skills you're looking for
  2. Structure the interview — base questions on actual job duties; use situational/behavioral/knowledge questions; use rating scales; use standardized form if possible
  3. Get organized — hold interview in a private room; minimize interruptions
  4. Establish rapport — greet the candidate warmly; start with a noncontroversial question; explain the timeframe
  5. Ask questions — follow the prepared situational, behavioral, and job-knowledge questions
  6. Take notes — brief, unobtrusive; helps avoid snap decisions; aids recall later
  7. Close the interview — leave time for candidate questions; advocate for your firm if appropriate
  8. Review the interview — immediately after candidate leaves, review notes, score answers, and make a decision while impressions are fresh

Three Ways to Make Interviews More Useful

1. Structure the interview — all applicants get the same questions; scored against consistent criteria

2. Carefully select traits to assess — only assess traits that are demonstrably relevant to job performance

3. Beware of interviewing errors — snap judgments, candidate-order errors, bias based on personal characteristics
